# 🛡️ Online Safety Guide for College Students

A simple and practical checklist to help students stay safe from cyber threats in everyday digital life.

---

## ✅ Good Practices to Follow

🔐 **Use Strong & Unique Passwords**  
Create different passwords for each account using a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.

🔒 **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)**  
Add an extra security layer to your accounts for better protection.

📧 **Be Cautious with Emails and Links**  
Always verify the sender before clicking on any link or downloading attachments.

🔄 **Keep Your Devices Updated**  
Regular updates fix security flaws and improve protection.

🛡️ **Install Trusted Antivirus Software**  
Use reliable security tools to detect and prevent threats.

📥 **Download from Safe Sources Only**  
Stick to official websites and app stores.

💳 **Protect Your UPI Credentials**  
Never share your UPI PIN or OTP with anyone.

✔️ **Verify Transactions Carefully**  
Double-check payment requests before approving them.

---

## ❌ Practices to Avoid

🚫 **Avoid Reusing Passwords**  
Using the same password increases the risk of multiple account breaches.

⚠️ **Don’t Ignore Security Alerts**  
Warnings are important—take them seriously.

🔗 **Avoid Clicking Unknown Links**  
Do not open links from unverified sources.

📦 **Avoid Pirated Software**  
Cracked apps often contain malware.

📶 **Don’t Use Public Wi-Fi for Payments**  
Unsecured networks can expose your data.

🔑 **Never Share OTP or UPI PIN**  
No legitimate service will ever ask for these details.

💰 **Don’t Accept Unknown Payment Requests**  
Scammers often use fake payment requests.

📢 **Limit Personal Information Online**  
Avoid oversharing on social media platforms.

---

## 🚨 Reporting Cybercrime

If you become a victim, report immediately:

- 🌐 **National Cyber Crime Portal:** https://cybercrime.gov.in  
- 📞 **Cyber Crime Helpline:** 1930  

---

## 💡 Final Note

> Staying alert and following basic cybersecurity practices daily is the best way to stay safe online.
